Day 39

Gdansk.

Today, we visited the Museum of the Second World War. This museum focused much on Poland's involvement during the war. Afterwards, a few of my peers expressed shock at how war permeats every aspect of one's life. I have to wonder if they hadn't made the connection prior about the US' crimes in the middle east.

The museum also focused much on the Soviet Union during WWII, which goes entirely unemphasized in US education.

After taking time to process this and recent events in the US, I hopped onboard a boat and watched the sunset from the Baltic Sea, befriending two women enjoying their weekend and dancing to folk music. Sometimes, you just need to listen to the water and be out in fresh, open air.

Day 36

Warsaw.

Warsaw has very interesting and variant architecture. Also, I spent all day stressed because I misread a single number.

I never in a million years expected to actually get into Tartu's program, and I'm going to have to take out a very small loan. This is absolutely nothing compared to what Miami is doing to me financially, so I'm pretty much incapable of stressing over that, but it's definitely going to be a busy moment while I'm back in the US between MUDEC and Tartu.

I need to change banks back in the states, too. The issues that I've had throughout this trip have been comical. This last incident was actually following a procedure that would make sense if not for one tiny detail, which is an oversight that I can (for once) understand. Each of the others was simply the bank not following clear, numerical instructions.

Me: So you've allowed usage for these two accounts that I hold, throughout the schengen area?

Banker: That is correct.

Truth: One card of two can now be used in four different schengen states.

Me: So, I'm just here to verify that both accounts can be used throughout the schengen area.

Banker: Oh! No, only the one is currently capable of that.

Me: So, now you've fixed this?

Banker: Yep! Both are now ready to go.

Me: Throughout all of the schengen area?

Banker: Yep!

Truth: Both cards can now be used in four different schengen states.

Me in Sicily: Hm. I can't use these cards here, and I am in a schengen state.

Banker: Oh, we only cleared access for four nations.

And now, of course, they accidentally locked me out of my accounts hopefully only until I'm back in Luxembourg and at worst, until I'm back in the US.

I got a recommendation to skip my week in centering in Utrecht and checking out different digital ethics MS programmes; definitely not following that recommendation. Changing the flight date would actually be more expensive than that entire week's living.

I am just a little pre-stressed about Dr. Rao's class starting during my finals week. Given financial restraints and how hardcore I hit travel during the program, I might use the weekend as my MUDEC study time; Rao's will almost definitely instantly take over during that week, but that still doesn't mean that I can just sit back on three entire other courses during their finals.

Taking Dr. Rao's class, working as a remote teaching assistant for Dr. Alomari in software engineering, and learning about how to construct digital governments and democracy well. During some and between others, the loan and another personal concern to handle. I don't subscribe to #GrindCulture, but sometimes it sure does feel like I accidentally picked it up during Spring 2021.

I have such a busy summer still ahead.

Warsaw, I look forward to focusing on you over all of that nonsense, today.
